## Purpose

The Ombuds Office is load-bearing across six patches. It is not a single-function watchdog — it is the independent verification layer that multiple other mitigations depend on. If the Ombuds Office is not constituted with explicit, written authority before deployment, at least three patches fail silently:

- **P-015** (T-018/T-019): requires the Ombuds to make a manufactured-flag determination within 4 hours. Without pre-defined criteria, this is an improvised judgment under maximum time pressure — easily wrong, easily challenged, and easily captured.
- **P-012** (T-012–T-015): requires Ombuds co-certification for PCRP activation during demand-context periods. Without formal authority, this certification is legally and institutionally meaningless.
- **P-008** (T-008): requires the Ombuds to track cohort concentrations in oversight bodies. Without mandate, this is surveillance the Ombuds has no basis to conduct.

The T-019 open problem states directly: *"Ombuds assessment of 'deliberate manufacture' requires judgment under time pressure. Criteria must be specified in advance in Annex AI (Ombuds Office constitution) with explicit authority and defined timeline."*

This annex constitutes the Ombuds Office. It defines mandate, independence requirements, decision criteria for manufactured-flag determinations, and the meta-capture prevention mechanism. The founding coalition must fill all **[FOUNDING COMMITMENT]** fields before deployment.

## Structure of this Annex

This annex specifies seven things:

1. **Mandate** — the full scope of Ombuds authority across all patches
2. **Independence requirements** — appointment, funding, recusal, and term structure
3. **Manufactured-flag authority** — the pre-launch blocking requirement for P-015; decision criteria, timeline, and conservative default

5. **Meta-capture prevention** — who audits the Ombuds, and how
6. **Publication commitment** — what the Ombuds publishes, how often, and in what form
7. **Founding coalition instructions** — what must be completed before deployment

## Section 1 — Mandate

The Ombuds Office holds a standing, independent mandate to:

1. **Monitor, assess, and certify** time-sensitive protocol events that require independent judgment under operational pressure — specifically, manufactured-flag determinations (P-015), PCRP co-certifications (P-012), and SQ-related assessments.
2. **Track concentration and cohort formation** in oversight bodies as required by P-008, and report publicly on epistemic diversity across all bodies required to meet the AC3.3 standard.
3. **Access the Restricted Threat Register Annex** and participate in annual consistency attestation as specified in Annex AO (P-021).
4. **Nominate or participate** in the P-014 founding panel as specified in Annex AH (one Ombuds officer, lot-drawn from staff; no veto power).
5. **Monitor and publish** AED compliance metrics as part of the Pillar 11 quarterly dashboard, in coordination with the identity system operators but independently of them (P-016 / Annex AK).
6. **Receive and triage** exclusion-rate reports from vulnerable-population advocacy organisations for identity system audit triggers (P-016 / Annex AK).
7. **Issue public notices** when any review trigger in Annex AK or Annex AR is exceeded.

The Ombuds mandate is explicitly **not** a veto over operational decisions. The Ombuds certifies, monitors, reports, and escalates. It does not block, approve, or authorize operations — with the single exception of manufactured-flag determinations under Section 3 below, where a finding of deliberate manufacture lifts a demand-context flag.

## Section 3 — Manufactured-Flag Authority

*This section is the primary pre-launch blocking requirement for P-015. It must be complete, with all [FOUNDING COMMITMENT] fields filled, before P-015 can be operative.*

### 3.1 — Authority Statement

The Ombuds Office holds explicit, standing authority to determine whether a demand-context flag (T-019 mechanism) was triggered by a manufactured or strategically timed enforcement action. This authority is pre-committed and does not require case-by-case authorization. Upon a finding of deliberate manufacture, the Ombuds lifts the demand-context designation; PCRP activation then proceeds at standard scope.

### 3.2 — Decision Criteria for "Deliberate Manufacture"

A demand-context flag is considered **deliberately manufactured** if *all three* of the following criteria are satisfied:

| Criterion | Standard |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Timing proximity** | The triggering enforcement action was initiated within **[FOUNDING COMMITMENT — e.g., 48 hours]** of a sentinel indicator movement of **[FOUNDING COMMITMENT — e.g., ≥15%]** in a high-volatility essential category |
| **Proportionality anomaly** | The enforcement action was materially smaller in scope or impact than the historical baseline for actions of its stated type — assessed against the prior **[FOUNDING COMMITMENT — e.g., 12 months]** of enforcement records for that category |
| **No documented operational basis** | The enforcement action lacks contemporaneous internal documentation explaining the timing — assessed against the enforcement body's own operational records, obtained under Ombuds access authority |

**Conservative default:** If the Ombuds cannot complete the assessment within the 4-hour timeline (Section 3.3), the demand-context designation is **maintained** while assessment continues. PCRP may activate at *reduced scope* (single-source authorization at 50% of standard maximum reduction) during the assessment period. This means a genuine supply shock is partially mitigated even if the Ombuds assessment is running — the conservative default does not leave the population unprotected, but also does not fully lift the flag on incomplete assessment.

**Threshold note:** The three-criterion standard is deliberately conjunctive — all three must be satisfied. This prevents the Ombuds from lifting a demand-context flag based on suspicious timing alone. Timing proximity is a signal; proportionality anomaly confirms it; absence of documented basis closes it. A single criterion does not trigger a finding of deliberate manufacture.

### 3.3 — Decision Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Cross-register alert | Automated — within 5 minutes of flag activation | Timing monitor flags the triggering enforcement action if within the proximity window; Ombuds office alerted automatically |
| Initial assessment | **[FOUNDING COMMITMENT — e.g., 1 hour]** | Ombuds investigator reviews enforcement records and sentinel indicator movement; applies proportionality criterion |
| Documentation review | **[FOUNDING COMMITMENT — e.g., 2 hours]** | Ombuds contacts enforcement body under access authority; reviews contemporaneous documentation |
| Determination | **[FOUNDING COMMITMENT — e.g., 1 hour]** | Commissioner or designated Deputy issues written determination: deliberate manufacture (flag lifted) or no finding (flag maintained) |
| **Total timeline** | **4 hours from flag activation** | Determination published immediately upon issuance; reasons stated |

If the enforcement body does not provide access to contemporaneous documentation within the documentation review window, the absence of documentation counts as satisfying the third criterion. This prevents obstruction via delay.

### 3.4 — Publication and Record

Every manufactured-flag determination — including determinations of no finding — is published immediately upon issuance as a public record. The publication includes:
- The three-criterion assessment with evidence basis
- The timeline from flag activation to determination
- The outcome (flag lifted or maintained) with effective time
- The name of the determining officer

A pattern of repeated no-findings in the same enforcement body is a Pillar 11 dashboard metric (Section 6).

## Section 4 — Other Load-Bearing Functions

### 4.1 — P-008 Cohort Concentration Tracking

The Ombuds tracks cohort concentrations in all oversight bodies required to meet the AC3.3 epistemic diversity standard. Specifically:
- Maintains a rolling cohort register of individuals with review authority over any subject in the same body
- Flags when any two cohort members share review authority over the same subject simultaneously
- Publishes quarterly diversity compliance reports per oversight body
- Triggers independent process audit when a 20-point legibility disparity is detected in any Pillar 8 verification category

**The Ombuds does not set diversity standards** — those are set by P-008. The Ombuds monitors compliance with the existing standard and publishes the results. This is the distinction between oversight and capture.

### 4.2 — P-012 PCRP Demand-Context Co-Certification

For PCRP activation during a demand-context period, the Ombuds co-certifies that:
1. The demand-context flag is valid (not found to be manufactured under Section 3)
2. The PCRP activation request is consistent with the actual supply signal
3. The proposed activation scope is within PCRP permitted parameters

Co-certification is required within **[FOUNDING COMMITMENT — e.g., 2 hours]** of activation request. If Ombuds cannot co-certify within the window, PCRP activates at reduced scope under single-source authorization — identical to the manufactured-flag conservative default.

### 4.3 — P-013/P-014 Founding Panel Participation

One Ombuds staff member participates in the P-014 heightened panel. This member is:
- Lot-drawn from staff with no prior involvement in the P-013 or P-014 design process
- Required to attest no affiliation with the design team within 5 years
- Bound by the standard adversarial member dissent-publication requirement

The Ombuds does not hold a veto on the P-014 panel; it holds one vote of five. Its participation is independence verification, not a blocking mechanism.

### 4.4 — P-016/Annex AK AED Monitoring

The Ombuds:
- Receives exclusion-rate reports from vulnerable-population advocacy organisations
- Publishes quarterly Pillar 11 dashboard metrics for fraud and exclusion rates
- Triggers independent audit when AED targets in Annex AK are exceeded
- Nominates a member to the annual AED recalibration panel

The Ombuds does not operate the identity system and has no role in its configuration. Its function is outcome monitoring — it observes what the system produces, not how it is tuned.

### 4.5 — Annex AO Register Access

Ombuds staff hold restricted access to the Threat Register Restricted Annex under Annex AO. Access is need-to-know within the office. Ombuds participates in the annual consistency attestation review.

## Section 5 — Meta-Capture Prevention

*The T-008 open problem states: "Ombuds Office carries three load-bearing P-008 functions — if Ombuds becomes elite formation site, all three fail simultaneously. Open question: who audits the auditors of elite formation?"*

The Ombuds cannot be its own auditor. This section answers the open question.

### 5.1 — External Oversight Body

An **Ombuds Oversight Panel** is constituted with the following authority:
- Annual review of Ombuds independence compliance (Section 2 requirements)
- Power to initiate removal proceedings if removal conditions in Section 2.4 are met
- Annual public report on Ombuds institutional health

**Panel composition:**
- **[FOUNDING COMMITMENT — e.g., 3 members: 1 nominated by civil liberties organisation with no CRP funding relationship; 1 nominated by independent academic institution with published constitutional law or public administration expertise; 1 lot-drawn from a published register of qualified independent practitioners with no prior employment relationship with the protocol administration]**
- Terms: **[FOUNDING COMMITMENT — e.g., 3 years, staggered; non-renewable]**

The Ombuds Oversight Panel is not part of the protocol administration structure. It is structurally external to the CRP, the founding coalition, and the Ombuds Office itself.

### 5.2 — Concentration Controls on the Ombuds

The Ombuds Office staff is subject to the same AC3.3 epistemic diversity standard it monitors in other bodies. Annual compliance is reported by the Ombuds Oversight Panel, not by the Ombuds itself.

No more than **[FOUNDING COMMITMENT — e.g., 25%]** of senior Ombuds staff may share institutional origin (defined as employment or primary affiliation within the prior 7 years at the same institution or its subsidiaries).

### 5.3 — Recusal Cascade

If the Ombuds Commissioner recuses, the senior Deputy acts. If both recuse, the Ombuds Oversight Panel appoints a temporary officer for the specific matter from its published register. The recusal cascade is pre-defined to prevent a gap in oversight function during high-pressure events.

## Section 6 — Publication Commitment

The following is published as part of the Ombuds Office public record:

| Metric | Publication format | Frequency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| All manufactured-flag determinations (findings and no-findings) | Full written determination with evidence basis, timeline, officer name | Immediately upon issuance |
| PCRP co-certifications (granted, denied, timeout) | Summary with outcome and timeline | Within 24 hours |
| Cohort concentration alerts (P-008) | Per-body compliance status | Quarterly |
| AED compliance status (P-016/AK) | Dashboard metrics per Annex AK Section 6 | Quarterly |
| Ombuds Oversight Panel annual report | Full report | Annually |
| Ombuds staff composition diversity report | AC3.3 compliance by dimension | Annually |
| Any recusal event | Officer, matter category, acting officer | Within 24 hours |

Publication of Ombuds activity — including all determination outcomes — is the mechanism that makes independence meaningful. If the Ombuds is systematically producing determinations that favor particular parties or bodies, the pattern is visible in the public record.
