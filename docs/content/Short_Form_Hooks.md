# Short-Form Hooks

These are written for short video openings, captions, or title cards. Keep delivery direct.

## Hooks

1. `What if losing money did not mean losing the basics?`
2. `What if markets stayed open, but survival stopped being for sale?`
3. `The same money that buys groceries should not buy government.`
4. `Poverty is not a law of nature. Bad system design is.`
5. `What if basic survival was a right, not a financial test?`
6. `A rich person can live better than you. They should not decide whether you eat.`
7. `This is not about ending markets. It is about ending coercion.`
8. `What if wealth could build a business, but not buy civic rule?`
9. `Most systems collapse survival, money, and power into one rail. That is the bug.`
10. `The Humane Constitution starts with one claim: survival should not depend on obedience or income.`

## Usage notes

- Hooks `1`, `2`, and `5` are best for broad audiences.
- Hooks `3`, `7`, and `9` are best for politically engaged audiences.
- Hooks `4`, `6`, and `10` are best when you want a sharper moral frame.

## Recommended follow-through

After the hook, move quickly to one of these sentences:

- `The design separates markets, survival, and civic power instead of forcing them through one form of money.`
- `That is why it uses Flow, Essential Access, Voice, Service Record, and Shared Storehouse as separate instruments.`
- `The whole idea is to stop wealth from automatically turning into coercion.`
