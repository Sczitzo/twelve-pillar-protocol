# FAQ Video Set

These are short source-grounded video answers built from the public docs. Each entry is designed for roughly 30 to 60 seconds on camera.

## 1. Is this socialism?

**Short answer:** No. The design does not abolish markets, trade, contracts, or enterprise. Flow still runs ordinary economic life.

**Three beats**

1. `Flow still handles wages, prices, contracts, saving, and investment.`
2. `The system limits what money can buy, especially survival access and civic power.`
3. `So the point is not central planning. The point is stopping wealth from purchasing coercion.`

## 2. Is this a social credit system?

**Short answer:** No. Voice and Service Record are bounded civic instruments, not a worth score.

**Three beats**

1. `They cannot buy goods, rights, survival access, or immunity.`
2. `The constitution explicitly rejects any measure of human worth.`
3. `They are for agenda-setting and readiness to serve in rotating public roles, not for ranking people.`

## 3. Can people still build wealth?

**Short answer:** Yes, but not in ways that let wealth dominate survival or civic rule.

**Three beats**

1. `People can still work, save, build firms, and invest through Flow.`
2. `What gets restricted is the ability to turn wealth into survival leverage or public control.`
3. `The design allows different outcomes, but not wealth-to-coercion conversion.`

## 4. What is Essential Access?

**Short answer:** It is the constitutional survival floor.

**Three beats**

1. `It covers baseline essentials like food, water, shelter, essential healthcare and medicines, and basic transit.`
2. `It is not cash and cannot be converted into ordinary money.`
3. `It exists so losing income does not mean losing dignity or basic safety.`

## 5. What happens during real shortage?

**Short answer:** The system can activate Shared Storehouse, a temporary emergency rationing instrument.

**Three beats**

1. `It is only supposed to activate during verified shortage.`
2. `It is public, time-limited, and challengeable.`
3. `The idea is to handle scarcity openly instead of letting panic prices decide who goes without.`

## 6. Who decides what counts as essential?

**Short answer:** Not one office, not one vendor, and not one hidden technical standard.

**Three beats**

1. `The basket is governed through public process, challenge paths, and reviewable definitions.`
2. `Measurement of real capacity sits under Article III and the basket itself is governed under survival rules.`
3. `The repo treats the basket as a permanent constitutional contest zone, not a fixed list beyond challenge.`

## 7. What stops corruption or capture?

**Short answer:** The design assumes capture attempts will happen and builds around that assumption.

**Three beats**

1. `It separates instruments so power cannot silently travel through one channel.`
2. `It uses public logs, review triggers, threat modeling, and patch history.`
3. `It is not trust-based. It is supposed to be challengeable at the rule, institution, and measurement layers.`

## 8. Is it ready to launch now?

**Short answer:** No.

**Three beats**

1. `The repo is strong as a design and documentation project.`
2. `It still needs pilot evidence, a proven non-coercive identity stack, and a legitimate founding coalition.`
3. `So the right next move is testing, critique, and evidence-building — not pretending the hard parts are already solved.`

## Delivery notes

- Lead with the short answer in the first sentence.
- Keep the tone serious and calm.
- Do not oversell certainty. The strongest public voice for this project is precise, not hype-heavy.
