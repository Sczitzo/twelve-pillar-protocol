# ANNEX N. Transition, Genesis, and Deployability Preconditions
**Purpose.** Steady-state architecture is not enough. The first 90 days of rollout can lock in capture for generations. This annex therefore expands the transition pathway, names who decides what at genesis, and states when the protocol is not yet legitimately deployable.

**Design principle: if the founding moment is captured, later safeguards inherit the capture.**

### N1. Minimum preconditions before launch
- **A cross-ideological legitimacy coalition** willing to submit itself to published constraints rather than governing by emergency charisma.
- **Manual fallback capacity** for food, shelter, identity continuity, and payment continuity during digital or institutional failure.
- **At least provisional review institutions**, ombuds capacity, and public-interest advocates able to hear emergency claims immediately.
- **A publishable essential-capacity baseline** with known weaknesses rather than a false claim of omniscient measurement.
- **Public education materials** sufficient for ordinary people to understand Flow, Essential Access, Voice, Service Record, Shared Storehouse, rights, appeals, and basic onboarding.

### N2. Genesis governance (first 90 days)
- **Days 0-30:** debt-collection standstill for essentials-linked households; continuity guarantees for food, shelter, care, transit, and emergency mental health stabilization; publication of initial basket v0; publication of initial Flow supply formula and conversion map; appointment by mixed lot of provisional CRP and provisional oracle quorum under founding-exclusion rules, external observers, and a public challenge window. The provisional CRP has zero Tier 1 authority during this window.
- **Days 31-60:** shadow accounting in selected pilot regions; publish stress tests; open comment and challenge windows; complete conflict screening for provisional institutions; prohibit major asset privatizations during founding window.
- **Days 61-90:** limited live cutover by region or sector only after readiness gate sign-off, public red-team report, and emergency manual fallback verification.
- **Any genesis institution** formed by appointment rather than ordinary process must sunset or face re-authorization through published review within eight quarters; no founding decision is permanent merely because it happened first.

### N3. Founding anti-capture rules
- **No single incumbent ministry**, bank coalition, major landlord bloc, major platform, dominant essential-goods vendor network, or donor bloc may nominate or control a majority of any provisional founding body.
- **Founding decisions** about the initial basket, initial Flow supply, first oracle quorum, and first review institutions require recorded reasons, minority reports, and public challenge windows.
- **Large legacy creditors**, dominant landlords, and major essential-goods vendors must be subject to heightened conflict disclosure during genesis.
- **The protocol must admit** when a founding compromise is provisional rather than pretending it is eternal truth.

### N4. Transition honesty and deployability
The protocol is not deployable merely because it is internally coherent. It becomes deployable only when legitimacy, continuity capacity, and review capacity exist simultaneously.
- **If preconditions are absent**, the correct action is phased groundwork - institution building, public education, local pilot systems, and capacity mapping - not false full-rollout theater.
- **Every rollout plan** must name what political struggle, coalition-building, or civic preparation is still missing. Silence on this point is itself a design failure.

### AA1 — Founding Constitutional Assembly (belongs here)

The founding period shall be anchored in a purpose-elected Constitutional Assembly rather than in a purely technocratic provisional body. The Assembly exists to provide democratic legitimacy for the foundational text, the first CRP selection process, and the initial anti-capture operating constraints.

- **Mandate:** ratify the founding constitutional text, authorize the first CRP selection through a published sortition process, ratify the first transition gates, and dissolve.
- **Term:** fixed at 18 to 24 months with no ordinary extension.
- **Eligibility firewall:** Assembly members may not hold a CRP seat or senior protocol office for 4 years after Assembly dissolution.
- **Technical expertise:** legacy-system experts may advise under conflict disclosure but may not control founding decisions by virtue of advisory status.
- **Sunset rule:** any founding decision taken by provisional bodies auto-expires after 8 quarters unless ratified through ordinary constitutional channels.
- **During the founding window**, the provisional CRP has process-protection authority only. It may screen for obvious Tier 1 violations, conflict breaches, and classification defects, but it may not permanently redefine Tier 1 meaning or authorize irreversible constitutional narrowing before ordinary institutions are live.

### AA2 — Pre-announcement Membrane Construction Phase (belongs here)

Before any public announcement of full conversion to the protocol's monetary architecture, the system must complete a membrane construction phase under existing legal authority. This phase exists to reduce the announcement-to-flight seam identified in prior reviews.

- **Gateway licensing**, beneficial-ownership reporting, anti-circumvention review rails, and external settlement controls must be operational before conversion announcement.
- **The obligation-classification body** that distinguishes speculative, mission-critical, protected, and transition-sensitive claims must be constituted before announcement and must publish its conflict rules in advance.
- **No conversion announcement** may proceed until the membrane readiness checklist, legal basis, and enforcement staffing minimums are certified through the transition gates defined elsewhere in the Protocol.

### AA5 — Interpretation of Earlier Weaker Wording (meta-rule)

If any earlier section of this Protocol implies a single civic weighting ledger, a purely technocratic founding path, or unrestricted IP accumulation by default, the controlling text is the constitution and the live annex set as of the most recent ratified version. Earlier weaker wording is superseded to the extent necessary to preserve anti-capture logic, democratic legitimacy, and knowledge-commons discipline.
