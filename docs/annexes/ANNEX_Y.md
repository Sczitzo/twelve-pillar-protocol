## Y0. Purpose and Scope

The Constitutional Survival Minimum (CSM) is the hard lower bound on life-access guaranteed to every personhood-verified resident. The broader LC essential basket (`Master_Protocol.md` §VIII) may vary in response to capacity, policy, and period-specific choices. The CSM does not. It is the floor below which LC cannot be narrowed by any ordinary process, governance actor, emergency order, or technical update.

The CSM is not a generous definition of a good life. It is the narrow, defensible minimum below which survival and dignity are structurally threatened. It is set deliberately at the threshold of physical viability plus a dignity margin — not at desirable comfort.

Every numerical value in this annex is a **Tier 1 Founding Commitment**. Changing any number below requires the full H-3 refounding authority process defined in the Master Protocol's amendment ladder. No emergency, scarcity declaration, fiscal pressure, or administrative reinterpretation may lower these values.

## Y1. CSM Basket — Per-Person Daily Minimums

The following minimums apply to every personhood-verified resident, regardless of contribution status, civic record, pause status, employment, or identity state above provisional personhood.

### Y1.1 Caloric sufficiency
- **Minimum: 2,100 kcal/person/day** for adults. Age-adjusted minimums published below.
- **Rationale:** WHO/Sphere Standards emergency minimum. Below this value, sustained metabolic deficit begins for a median adult.
- **Age-adjustments (non-discretionary):**
  - Infants 0–6 months: breastfeeding support OR formula equivalent to 108 kcal/kg body weight/day.
  - Children 6 months–2 years: breastfeeding continuation OR 98 kcal/kg/day with protein complementation.
  - Children 2–5 years: 1,250 kcal/day minimum.
  - Children 6–12 years: 1,750 kcal/day minimum.
  - Adolescents 13–17 years: 2,400 kcal/day minimum.
  - Adults 18+: 2,100 kcal/day minimum (2,400 kcal for verified physical-labor contexts).
  - Pregnant: +300 kcal/day above adult baseline (2,400 kcal minimum).
  - Lactating: +500 kcal/day above adult baseline (2,600 kcal minimum).
- **Nutritional composition floor:** protein ≥50 g/day for adults (scaled for age); essential micronutrient baseline per WHO RDA for the consumed basket composition.

### Y1.2 Potable water
- **Minimum: 50 L/person/day total usage**, of which ≥7.5 L must be safe for drinking and cooking.
- **Rationale:** WHO basic-access threshold for health. 7.5 L drinking/cooking meets physiological survival + hydration; 50 L total covers hygiene, sanitation, and household needs at dignity-preserving scale.
- **Emergency substitution (Scarcity L2/L3 only):** floor may shift to 15 L/person/day under Sphere emergency standards with the following guarantees: ≥3 L safe for drinking; ≥2 L for cooking; ≥10 L for minimum hygiene/sanitation. Emergency substitution triggers mandatory capacity-recovery timeline publication per §Y3.

### Y1.3 Weather-safe shelter
- **Minimum floor space: 3.5 m²/person** of enclosed, weather-sealed habitable space (Sphere Standards emergency minimum).
- **Temperature envelope: 16°C–28°C** maintained indoor temperature during occupied hours. For regions outside this climatic band, active heating or cooling is a CSM obligation, not a discretionary amenity.
- **Humidity:** non-condensing; prevention of chronic damp and mold.
- **Structural safety:** resistant to locally expected weather severity (wind, precipitation, seismic per regional classification).
- **Sleep surface:** one elevated sleeping surface per person (no floor sleeping as default).
- **Lighting:** natural light access AND supplementary lighting adequate for reading after dark.
- **Privacy:** separable rooms or partitions available for adults of opposite sex beyond household unit, and for adolescents from guardians.

### Y1.4 Emergency medical stabilization
- **Response time floor: 60 minutes** from call to qualified responder arrival for urban density; 4 hours maximum for most-rural settlement categories.
- **Stabilization scope:** airway, breathing, circulation (ABC) stabilization; hemorrhage control; shock management; acute pain management; obstetric emergency response; neonatal resuscitation; poisoning/overdose response.
- **Cost to patient at point-of-service: zero.** No payment, no co-pay, no ID-gating beyond personhood verification (or provisional personhood for emergency cases).
- **Continuity post-stabilization:** transfer to full-care facility with continuous care chain; no discharge-to-street under any circumstance.

### Y1.5 Basic sanitation and hygiene
- **Sanitation:** access to a functioning toilet within 50 m of habitation, OR one toilet per 20 persons in shared facilities (Sphere Standards). Faecal matter containment and safe treatment.
- **Handwashing:** soap and running water available within 10 m of toilet and within kitchen/food-prep area.
- **Menstrual hygiene:** menstrual products available without cost or attestation for all menstruating persons, in privacy-preserving distribution.
- **Waste management:** solid-waste collection at least weekly; hazardous-waste separate channel; drainage preventing pooled contaminated water.
- **Personal hygiene supplies:** soap, toothpaste, toothbrush, menstrual products, shaving/grooming basics at quantity adequate for continuous use.

### Y1.6 Crisis mental-health stabilization
- **Crisis access:** 24/7 crisis contact (call/text/in-person) with qualified responder. Response to in-person crisis contact ≤4 hours.
- **Stabilization scope:** suicide and self-harm crisis response; acute psychosis stabilization; acute trauma response; dignity-preserving de-escalation; assisted access to continuing care.
- **Coercion prohibition:** mental-health stabilization services may never be used to gate LC, civic standing, residency, or personhood verification. Involuntary hold powers are narrowly tied to imminent self-harm or harm-to-others and subject to Pillar 10 due-process review within 72 hours.

### Y1.7 Essential medicines continuity
- **Scope:** WHO Model List of Essential Medicines, current edition, as floor. Additional medicines added by regional published formulary cannot be removed without substitution equivalence.
- **Continuity: 30-day supply minimum on-hand** for any person with a diagnosed condition requiring the medicine. Re-dispensation pipeline must prevent any gap.
- **Pediatric and pregnancy-adjusted formulations:** required where standard adult form is unsafe or unavailable at correct dose.
- **No cost at point-of-dispensation.**

## Y2. CSM Issuance Rule (Architecturally Enforced)

CSM issuance is not a governance decision. It is a mechanical function of personhood verification.

- **Trigger:** valid personhood commitment (Pillar 2) including provisional personhood states.
- **Mechanism:** ledger-layer automatic LC tranche issuance against CSM basket per 72-hour rolling window. Governance actors — including the Constitutional Review Panel, Regional Executive Body, Ombuds Office, and any emergency authority — are architecturally *unable* to halt CSM issuance for any verified personhood.
- **Identity uncertainty does not halt CSM.** Provisional personhood receives the humane-floor subset of the CSM basket while verification is in progress.
- **Scarcity does not halt CSM.** During Scarcity L2/L3, the CSM is preserved by invoking the substitution rules in §Y3. Capacity below CSM × population is a declared System Failure triggering §Y4.
- **Sanction does not halt CSM.** No judicial, regulatory, criminal, or contractual process may produce withdrawal of CSM access. Enforcement acts on EC, DW, CR, liberty (per due process), or civic privileges — never on CSM.
- **Administrative error does not halt CSM.** Any system action that reduces CSM below §Y1 minimums to any personhood-verified resident is, by definition, a Critical Incident (§Y4) regardless of intent.

## Y3. Scarcity Substitution Protocol

The CSM is preserved even during declared scarcity by substitution, not by narrowing.

- **Caloric substitution:** emergency food baskets with nutrient equivalence. Composition may vary by available supply; total kcal and protein floors may not fall below §Y1.1.
- **Water substitution:** emergency minimum 15 L/day as specified in §Y1.2. Mandatory restoration timeline published within 72 hours of entering substitution mode.
- **Shelter substitution:** temporary shared habitation at reduced sqm/person is permitted only when (a) individual dignity protections are preserved (privacy partitions, separate sleeping for vulnerable persons); (b) timeline to §Y1.3 restoration is published; (c) duration does not exceed 90 days without external review trigger under Pillar 3.
- **Medical and mental-health stabilization: NO substitution.** Response time floors and stabilization scope are invariant.
- **Medicines substitution:** therapeutic equivalents may be substituted only with prescriber review; no substitution is permitted where evidence of equivalence is absent.

All substitutions are published in real-time on the Pillar 11 dashboard with entry/exit timestamps, scope, and expected restoration timeline.

## Y4. CSM Failure as Critical Incident

Any observed failure to deliver the §Y1 minimums to any personhood-verified resident is a Critical Incident.

- **Mandatory disclosure:** published on Pillar 11 dashboard within 24 hours.
- **Mandatory investigation:** Ombuds Office (post-Proposal 8 federation) opens an investigation within 72 hours.
- **Mandatory restitution:** the affected person receives retroactive CSM provision, plus dignity-preserving redress, within 7 days of confirmation.
- **Mandatory root-cause review:** capacity gap, process failure, or capture is published within 30 days; patches registered per Patch Log.
- **Mandatory pattern detection:** if more than `[ACCEPTABLE_CSM_FAILURE_THRESHOLD]` (Tier 2 Founding Commitment; see `/founding/commitments.md`) failures occur in any jurisdictional cluster within a 30-day window, the protocol enters Scarcity L3 for that cluster regardless of oracle status, and H-3 refounding review is initiated at the next scheduled opportunity.

## Y5. Amendment and Lock

- **Every numerical value above is locked** by cryptographic hash commitment at founding (Proposal 1 architectural enforcement). Hash appears in `/architecture/drift_chain.md` and is referenced in the ledger genesis block.
- **Amendment requires H-3 refounding authority** as defined in the Master Protocol amendment ladder. No Tier 2 or Tier 3 process may modify §Y1.
- **Upward amendment** (increasing floors) is permitted through ordinary Tier 2 process. Only *downward* changes require H-3 refounding.
- **Temporary suspension prohibited.** No emergency, treaty, budget cycle, or transition process may suspend CSM. If capacity cannot meet CSM × population, the protocol is in System Failure and Y4 applies.

## Y6. Relationship to Broader Essential Basket

The essential basket defined in `Master_Protocol.md` §VIII is broader than the CSM. Ordinary policy, scarcity mode, or procurement choice may adjust the broader basket. The CSM is the subset of the broader basket that is invariant.

Example — the broader basket may include "basic clothing" or "basic local transit." These are essential-basket items but not CSM items. They may vary by region or period. The CSM items (food, water, shelter, emergency medicine, sanitation, mental-health stabilization, essential-medicines continuity) may not.

## Y7. Interaction with Other Pillars

- **Pillar 2 (Identity):** CSM applies to provisional personhood, full personhood, and civic-activated personhood alike. Identity uncertainty reduces civic powers, not CSM access.
- **Pillar 3 (Capacity):** the RCS publishes CSM × population estimates continuously. Any projected shortfall triggers §Y3 substitution planning immediately — not when the shortfall arrives.
- **Pillar 10 (Operations):** enforcement actions are architecturally prohibited from targeting CSM channels. Account partitioning (per Master §Pillar 5 wallet partitioning) ensures enforcement on EC or other channels does not touch LC/CSM.
- **Pillar 11 (Transparency):** CSM delivery rate, substitution events, and Critical Incidents are published in aggregate at the jurisdictional-cluster level. Individual-level CSM data is never published.
- **Pillar 12 (Resilience):** resilience reserves are sized to cover CSM × population × [RESERVE_WINDOW_DAYS] (Tier 2 Founding Commitment).
